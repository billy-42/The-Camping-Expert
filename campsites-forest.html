Best Places to Camp During Different Seasons
As the seasons wax and wane, the places you camp at probably should, too. In California, camping along beaches is most often done during summer, when the climate is warmest, but it can be done during winter. Camping during fall and early winter is not a good idea because of the intense amount of rain during those times. Camping inland can again be done any time of year. Summer camping is more comfortable, but winter camping (or doing snow campouts) can be a very interesting and challenging experience.
How Much do Reservations Cost?
Reservations vary widely from park to park. However, they will generally run from between $10 to $50. Most places are booked several weeks into the future, so planning a camping trip means planning and reserving ahead. Places like Yosemite are extremely busy, often having waiting lists of over a year and lotteries to get into the park at all. Compared to the hastle it takes to get into some parks, the reservation fee is nothing.
